# Results

## Genome-wide Distribution of Introgression Deserts

We identified 77 discrete genomic regions exhibiting consistent resistance to *S. tergeminus* ancestry across Iowa samples (≥80% of individuals classified as *S. catenatus* ancestry; hybrid index <0.1). These introgression deserts totaled 95.4 Mb, representing approximately 5% of the estimated *Sistrurus* genome (~2 Gb). Region length ranged from 200 kb to 12.69 Mb (mean ± SD: 1.24 ± 1.52 Mb; median: 650 kb). Gene content per desert ranged from 0 to 53 annotated genes (mean ± SD: 10.8 ± 11.4 genes; median: 7 genes). The largest desert (CM078120.1: 48.27–60.96 Mb; 12.69 Mb) contained 53 genes and exhibited the highest population-level ancestry support (mean support = 0.998; mean hybrid index = 0.005).

Deserts were distributed across 12 chromosomes, with three chromosomes (CM078115.1, CM078120.1, CM078121.1) harboring disproportionate numbers of large deserts (>2 Mb). CM078120.1 contained the most desert sequence (23.1 Mb across 10 regions), followed by CM078117.1 (13.4 Mb across 7 regions) and CM078121.1 (14.2 Mb across 12 regions). Twenty-three deserts (30%) lacked annotated genes, likely reflecting incomplete genome annotation or selection on non-coding regulatory elements.
